# Options Market Structure: Single-Stock Monday/Wednesday Expirations (2026)

## Key Finding

**January 26, 2026** — CBOE began listing Monday and Wednesday expirations (including 0DTE) for qualifying single-stock equity options. This is the first time individual stocks had non-Friday weekly expirations. Prior to this, only index products (SPX, SPY, QQQ) had daily or expanded expiration calendars.

## Qualifying Securities (Initial List)

TSLA, NVDA, AAPL, AMZN, META, MSFT, GOOGL, AVGO, IBIT

The list is evaluated quarterly by CBOE based on market cap and liquidity thresholds.

## Index vs Single-Stock Timeline (Critical Distinction)

| Product | Daily/Expanded Expirations | Source |
|---------|---------------------------|--------|
| SPX index | Wed added Oct-Nov 2022; Mon added Jun-Jul 2023; full daily by early 2024 | CBOE regulatory circulars |
| SPY, QQQ ETFs | Similar expansion 2022-2024 | Exchange notices |
| **Single stocks (AMZN, AAPL, TSLA, etc.)** | **Monday and Wednesday ONLY — effective January 26, 2026** | CBOE notice + Federal Register |

## Implications for Day Traders

- **Thin liquidity**: Product is only ~5 months old as of June 2026. OI on Mon/Wed strikes is significantly lower than Friday weeklies.
- **Wider spreads**: Market makers still calibrating pricing models for these new single-stock daily expirations.
- **Theta compression**: Short DTE options on these days have less established dealer positioning, making gamma behavior less predictable.
- **Not the same as SPX 0DTE**: The index 0DTE market has years of established flow and deep OI. Single-stock Mon/Wed products do not.

## What Changed vs Before

Before Jan 26, 2026, AMZN single-stock options only had:
- Standard monthly expirations (3rd Friday)
- Friday weekly expirations (Weeklys program, started ~2010s for individual stocks)

There were NO Monday or Wednesday single-stock option expiration dates before January 26, 2026.
