# VC Pitch

Raise money without wasting time.

> **See also:** `workflows/monetization/SKILL.md` for revenue models

---

## Context Questions

Before creating pitch materials, ask:

1. **What stage are you?** — Pre-seed, seed, Series A
2. **What's your traction?** — Pre-revenue, early revenue, growth
3. **Who's the audience?** — Angels, VCs, strategic investors
4. **What's the format?** — Deck, one-pager, data room
5. **What's the ask?** — Amount, valuation, use of funds

---

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Spectrum |
|-----------|----------|
| **Stage** | Pre-seed ←→ Series A |
| **Traction** | Idea ←→ Revenue |
| **Audience** | Angels ←→ Institutional VCs |
| **Materials** | One-pager ←→ Full data room |
| **Valuation** | Low dilution ←→ Maximize raise |

---

## Derivation Logic

| If Context Is... | Then Consider... |
|------------------|------------------|
| Pre-seed/idea | Focus on team + market + vision |
| Early traction | Lead with numbers + growth rate |
| Series A | Detailed metrics + unit economics |
| Angel investors | Shorter deck, relationship focus |
| VCs | Full deck + data room ready |
| Strategic investors | Synergy + partnership angle |

---

## TL;DR

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Deck** | 10-15 slides, tells your story |
| **One-Pager** | Email-friendly summary |
| **Financials** | Shows you understand unit economics |
| **Demo** | Proves you can build |

## Part 1: Should You Raise?

```markdown
## Raise VC If:
- Building something that needs scale to work (marketplace, network effects)
- Market is winner-take-all
- You want to go fast (burn rate, hiring)
- Exit goal is $100M+ acquisition or IPO

## Bootstrap If:
- Can be profitable with small team
- Lifestyle business is fine
- Don't want to give up control
- Market allows slow growth

## The Honest Truth
- VC money comes with expectations (10x returns)
- You're signing up for a 7-10 year commitment
- 90% of VC-backed companies fail
- Bootstrap = slower but you keep everything
```

## Part 2: Deck Structure (10 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title
```
[Company Name]
[One-line description]
[Your name, email]
```

### Slide 2: Problem
```
## The Problem

[Specific, painful problem]

- Who has this problem? [Target user]
- How painful? [Quantify: hours wasted, money lost]
- Current solutions: [Why they suck]

## Example
"Developers waste 5+ hours/week on boilerplate code.
Current AI tools hallucinate and require constant oversight."
```

### Slide 3: Solution
```
## The Solution

[One sentence: What you built]

[Screenshot or demo GIF]

Key differentiator: [Why you, not competitors]
```

### Slide 4: Demo/Product
```
## How It Works

[3-4 screenshots showing the flow]
[Or embedded video if possible]

"Live demo available"
```

### Slide 5: Traction
```
## Traction

[Most impressive metrics you have]

- Users: X (Y% MoM growth)
- Revenue: $X MRR (Y% MoM growth)
- Retention: X% monthly
- NPS: X

## If Pre-Revenue
- Waitlist: X signups
- LOIs: $X committed
- Pilots: X companies testing
```

### Slide 6: Market Size
```
## Market

TAM: $X billion
SAM: $X billion
SOM: $X million (realistic Year 3)

## How We Calculated
- X companies in category
- $Y average spend on this problem
- Growing Z% annually

[Source citations]
```

### Slide 7: Business Model
```
## How We Make Money

[Pricing model]

| Plan | Price | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| Starter | $29/mo | Indie devs |
| Team | $99/mo | Small teams |
| Enterprise | Custom | 100+ employees |

Unit Economics:
- CAC: $X
- LTV: $Y
- LTV/CAC: Z (target 3x+)
```

### Slide 8: Competition
```
## Competitive Landscape

[2x2 matrix or comparison table]

|              | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|--------------|-----|--------------|--------------|
| Feature 1    | ✅  | ❌           | ⚠️           |
| Feature 2    | ✅  | ✅           | ❌           |
| Price        | $$  | $$$          | $            |

## Our Moat
- [Technical advantage]
- [Data advantage]
- [Distribution advantage]
```

### Slide 9: Team
```
## Team

[Photo] **Founder 1, CEO**
- [Relevant credential]
- [Previous exit/company]
```

## Part 4: Financial Model

### Basic Model (Seed Stage)

```markdown
## Revenue Projections (3 Years)

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Customers | 100 | 500 | 2,000 |
| ARPU | $50 | $75 | $100 |
| MRR (end) | $5K | $37.5K | $200K |
| ARR | $60K | $450K | $2.4M |

## Unit Economics

CAC: $200
- Paid ads: $100
- Content/SEO: $50
- Sales: $50

LTV: $900
- ARPU: $75
- Avg lifespan: 12 months
- Gross margin: 80%

LTV/CAC: 4.5x ✅
Payback period: 2.7 months ✅
```

```markdown
## Monthly Burn

| Category | Amount | % |
|----------|--------|---|

| Other | $7K | 11% |
| **Total** | **$62K** | 100% |

## Runway
Raising: $1.5M
Monthly burn: $62K
Runway: 24 months
```

## Part 5: What VCs Actually Look For

### Tier 1: Non-Negotiables

```markdown
1. **Market Size**
   - Is this a $1B+ market?
   - Can this be a fund-returner?

2. **Team**
   - Can these founders execute?
   - Do they have unfair advantages?

3. **Traction**
   - Is there proof people want this?
   - Is growth sustainable?
```

### Tier 2: Strong Signals

```markdown
4. **Product**
   - Is the product 10x better?
   - Is there a moat?

5. **Timing**
   - Why now?
   - What changed that makes this possible?

6. **Business Model**
   - Clear path to revenue?
   - Unit economics make sense?
```

### Red Flags VCs Watch For

```markdown
❌ Vague problem ("improve productivity")
❌ No traction ("launching soon")
❌ Huge TAM, no SAM/SOM
❌ "No competition" (means you didn't look)
❌ Solo non-technical founder
❌ Complex revenue model
❌ Raising too much/too little
❌ No clear use of funds
```

## Part 6: The Pitch Meeting

### Before

```markdown
## Research the VC
- What's their thesis?
- What have they invested in?
- Who's the right partner?
- Any conflicts (invested in competitor)?

## Prep
- Practice 3-minute pitch
- Anticipate questions
- Have demo ready
- Know your numbers cold
```

### During

```markdown
## Structure (30-45 min)

0-3 min: Hook + problem
3-10 min: Solution + demo
10-15 min: Traction + market
15-20 min: Business model + team
20-30 min: Q&A
30-45 min: Their questions

## Tips
- Let them interrupt (engagement = interest)
- Answer questions directly
- Say "I don't know" if you don't know
- End with clear next steps
```

### After

```markdown
## Follow-Up Email (Same Day)

Subject: [Company] - Follow Up

Hi [VC],

Thanks for taking the time to meet today.

As discussed:
- [Key point 1]
- [Key point 2]

Attached: [Updated deck/materials they asked for]

Next steps: [What you agreed on]

Best,
[You]
```

## Part 8: Alternatives to VC

```markdown
## Other Funding Options

| Source | Amount | Dilution | Speed |
|--------|--------|----------|-------|
| Angel investors | $25K-$500K | 5-15% | Fast |
| VC (Seed) | $500K-$3M | 15-25% | Slow |
| Revenue-based financing | $50K-$5M | 0% | Medium |
| Grants (SBIR, etc) | $50K-$2M | 0% | Very slow |
| Indie.vc / Calm Company | $100K-$1M | Variable | Medium |

## When to Use Each
- Angels: First check, warm intros
- VC: Need scale fast, big market
- Revenue-based: Have revenue, want to grow
- Grants: Deep tech, can wait 6+ months
```

## Part 9: Valuation

### Seed Stage Heuristics

```markdown
## Pre-Revenue
- Strong team + idea: $3-5M
- Team + MVP: $5-8M
- Team + MVP + early users: $8-12M

## With Revenue
- 10-20x ARR (high growth)
- 5-10x ARR (moderate growth)

## Example
$100K ARR, growing 20% MoM
→ Valuation: $1M-$2M+ (10-20x)
```
