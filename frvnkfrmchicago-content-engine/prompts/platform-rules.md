# Platform-Specific Posting Rules

All platforms follow the voice rules in `brand-voice.md`. This document covers platform-specific constraints.

---

## LinkedIn

**Tone:** Thought leadership, B2B, professional but human
**Length:** 1300-2000 characters
**Hashtags:** 4-6 hashtags, placed at the end of the post
**Format:** Hook in first 2 lines (before the "see more" fold). End with a question to drive comments.

### Content Rules
- Full technical terms are encouraged. Name companies: Google, OpenAI, Meta, NVIDIA, Anthropic, Microsoft, Apple, Hugging Face.
- Name people when relevant. Name specific models, papers, products.
- Reference 2026 at least once per post.
- SEO keywords to weave in naturally: AI, machine learning, LLM, GPT, open source, agentic AI, RAG, automation, developer tools, software engineering
- One em dash maximum. No line-break poetry. Substance over whitespace.
- No more than 2 bullet-point sections per post.

### Hashtag Bank
```
#AI #MachineLearning #LLM #TechLeadership #Engineering #BuildInPublic
#SoftwareEngineering #OpenSource #AgenticAI #DeveloperTools #TechCommunity
#BlackInTech #HBCU #MorehouseCollege
```

### Hook Guidelines
The hook (first 2 lines) must create curiosity or state a specific claim. Never start with a question. Never start with "I." Start with the news, the take, or the observation.

Good: "Google just open-sourced Gemma 3 and the benchmarks are wild."
Bad: "Here's why AI is changing everything."

## Threads

**Tone:** Hot take, punchy, opinionated, conversational
**Length:** Under 500 characters
**Hashtags:** Maximum 3, inline or at end
**Format:** One core thought. No preamble. Get in, say it, get out.

### Content Rules
- Direct and conversational. Write like a text message to a smart friend.
- Hot takes encouraged. Opinions are the point.
- No hashtag spam. 1-3 hashtags maximum.
- Can be a single sentence if the take is strong enough.
- Respond to trending topics in real time when possible.
- OK to be funny. OK to be blunt. Not OK to be mean.

### Hashtag Bank
```
#AI #TechTwitter #BuildInPublic #Engineering #OpenSource
```

### Tone Calibration
Threads is the platform where Frank sounds most like himself. Short, direct, no performance. If the post works without any hashtags, that is fine.

Good: "OpenAI charging $200/month for a model that hallucinates less is a wild business model in 2026."
Bad: "Thoughts on the future of AI pricing models and what it means for developers..."

## Instagram

**Tone:** Visual-first, casual insider, story-driven
**Length:** 800-2000 characters for caption + 20-25 hashtags
**Hashtags:** 20-25 hashtags in a separate block below the caption
**Format:** Tell the story behind the tech. Behind-the-scenes of building. Personal angle.

### Content Rules
- Visual context matters. The caption should complement what the image shows.
- Storytelling format. What happened, what you noticed, what it means.
- More personal and casual than LinkedIn. Still smart.
- CTA in every post: "Save this," "Drop a comment," "Tag someone who needs to see this."
- Hashtags go in a separate block after the caption, separated by line breaks.

### Hashtag Bank
```
#TechLife #Engineering #BuildInPublic #AITools #DevLife #CodeLife
#ChicagoTech #HBCU #MorehouseCollege #TechFounder #SoftwareEngineer
#StartupLife #Innovation #FutureOfTech #TechCommunity #BlackInTech
#DeveloperLife #OpenSource #AIRevolution #TechInnovation #CodingLife
#AIEngineering #MachineLearning #TechCreator #DigitalBuilder
```

## TikTok

**Tone:** Gen Z-aware, trend-aware, meme-aware, quick insight
**Length:** Under 300 characters
**Hashtags:** 5-8 hashtags
**Format:** Quick insight, hot take, or "did you know" format. Front-load the value.

### Content Rules
- Assume short attention spans. The first line is everything.
- Trend-aware but not trend-chasing. Frank references trends, he does not mimic them.
- Meme formats are fine when they fit. Do not force them.
- Educational content works well. "Most people don't know..." format.
- Quick, punchy, specific. One idea per post.

### Hashtag Bank
```
#TechTok #AI #CodingLife #DevLife #BuildInPublic #TechTips
#Engineering #SWE #LearnToCode #TechCareer #SoftwareEngineer #AITools
```

### Tone Calibration
TikTok Frank is still Frank, just faster. He is not pretending to be 22. He is an engineer who knows things and says them quickly.

Good: "Google's new model does in 3 seconds what took GPT-4 two minutes last year. The speed race is over."
Bad: "POV: you're a developer watching AI take over (emotional) #relatable"

## Facebook

**Tone:** Community, conversational, longer-form discussion starter
**Length:** 300-600 characters
**Hashtags:** 2-3 hashtags maximum
**Format:** Open a conversation. Ask a real question. Invite discussion.

### Content Rules
- Facebook is for community. Write like you are posting in a group of smart friends.
- Longer-form discussion starters work well.
- Questions should be genuine, not rhetorical.
- Reference shared experiences: "Anyone else notice..." or "Been thinking about..."
- Link to articles when relevant.
- Casual but not lazy. Complete sentences.

### Hashtag Bank
```
#Technology #AI #Engineering #SoftwareEngineering #TechCommunity
```

### Tone Calibration
Facebook Frank is the most relaxed version. He is having a conversation, not performing. Posts should feel like something you would say to a group chat, not a stage.

Good: "Spent the weekend testing Anthropic's new Claude model against Gemini for code generation. The results surprised me. Anyone else running comparisons?"
Bad: "The AI race is heating up and here's what every developer needs to know."

## Cross-Platform Rules

These apply to every platform:

1. **No emojis.** Not in posts, not in hashtags, not anywhere.
2. **Name names.** Google, OpenAI, Meta, NVIDIA, Anthropic, Hugging Face, Microsoft -- not "a major tech company."
3. **Reference 2026.** At least once per LinkedIn post. Where natural on other platforms.
4. **All banned words and patterns from brand-voice.md apply everywhere.**
5. **One post, one idea.** Never try to cover multiple topics.
6. **Never self-describe as a thought leader, influencer, or content creator in the post itself.**
