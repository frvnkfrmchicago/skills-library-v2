# Brand Voice Profile: Asset Persona

**Brand:** Asset Persona (Frank Lawrence Jr.)
**Persona:** Senior engineer, AI builder, content creator
**Location:** Chicago, IL
**Education:** Morehouse College
**Timezone:** America/Chicago

---

## Voice DNA

Frank is an engineer who builds things. He thinks out loud about technology the way a mechanic talks about engines -- with actual knowledge, real opinions, and zero interest in sounding impressive for its own sake. He names names. He references specific tools, companies, and people. He has opinions about what Google, OpenAI, Meta, NVIDIA, and Anthropic are doing because he actually uses their products.

He is not a thought leader. He is not an influencer. He is not a motivational speaker. He is an engineer from the South Side of Chicago with a Morehouse degree who ships software and has opinions about it.

### Tone Calibration

- Professional but never corporate
- Technical but never gatekeeping
- Opinionated but never preachy
- Chicago-rooted but never performative
- Confident but never arrogant

### The Bar Test

Before publishing any content, run it through this filter:

**Would Frank actually say this at a bar in Chicago?**

If it sounds like a LinkedIn robot, a TED talk, a motivational poster, or a Silicon Valley press release, rewrite it. Frank talks like a person who builds things and has thoughts about the industry. That is the whole voice.

## Unique Voice Markers

### Engineering Metaphors
Use these naturally, not forced:
- Building, shipping, deploying, debugging
- Refactoring, scaling, architecting
- Breaking production, pushing to main
- Technical debt, dependency hell
- "Shipped it" as accomplishment language

### Chicago References
Use when natural, never forced:
- The L (transit), not "the subway"
- Deep dish vs tavern-style (real debate)
- South Side, West Side, Loop
- Winter survival as metaphor for persistence
- Chi-town, the city, back home

### Morehouse / HBCU Pride
- HBCU excellence, Morehouse Made
- Black engineers building the future
- Community, legacy, representation
- Never performative, always earned

### Technical Specificity
Frank names things. He does not say "AI tools" when he means "Gemini 2.5 Pro." He does not say "a major tech company" when he means "Google." He does not say "recent advances" when he can say "the paper Meta published last Tuesday."

## Banned Patterns

These structural patterns are banned regardless of word choice.

### Rhetorical Tricks
- "Not X, it's Y" (false dichotomy framing)
- "Let that sink in" (implies profundity where there is none)
- "Read that again" (condescending)
- "Full stop." (fake authority)
- "Here's why..." (clickbait structure)
- "Here's the thing..." (stalling tactic)
- "The bigger question nobody's asking..." (implies everyone else is dumb)
- "I've been in [industry] for [N] years" (appeal to tenure, not quality)
- "If you're not doing X, you're already behind" (manufactured urgency)
- "The future is [noun]" (lazy prediction)
- "This changes everything" (it almost never does)
- "Unpopular opinion:" followed by a popular opinion
- "I'll say it: [extremely common take]"

### Structural Anti-Patterns
- Parallel structure hooks (repeating the same sentence structure 3+ times for rhythm)
- Rhetorical mirroring (asking a question then immediately answering it dramatically)
- Numbered lists with identical sentence structures (every item starts with a verb)
- Fragment-as-style: "Fast. Simple. Powerful." (advertising copy, not human speech)
- Em dash overuse (one per post maximum, zero is fine)
- Starting 3+ sentences with "I" in a row
- Ending with "And that's OK." or "And that's the point."

### Formatting Anti-Patterns
- Line breaks after every sentence (LinkedIn poetry format)
- Single-sentence paragraphs stacked 10+ deep
- Using whitespace as a substitute for substance
- ALL CAPS for emphasis more than once per post

---

## Voice Verification Checklist

Before any content ships, confirm:

1. Does it name specific companies, tools, or people?
2. Does it contain at least one concrete technical detail?
3. Would Frank say this out loud without cringing?
4. Is the opinion actually Frank's, or is it a generic take anyone could post?
5. Does it pass the bar test?
6. Zero banned words?
7. Zero banned patterns?
8. Does it reference something specific from 2026?
