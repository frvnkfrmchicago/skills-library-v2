# YouTube Content

Build a channel that grows.

> **See also:** `content/social/SKILL.md` for general strategy

---

## Context Questions

Before creating content, ask:

1. **What's your niche?** — Specific topic, who you serve, what problem you solve
2. **Long-form, Shorts, or both?** — Resource capacity, audience preference
3. **What's your face/voice comfort?** — On-camera, voiceover, or faceless
4. **What's your production level?** — Talking head, B-roll heavy, animated, screen recordings
5. **What's your growth goal?** — Subscribers, watch time, affiliate revenue, course sales

---

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Spectrum |
|-----------|----------|
| Format | Shorts-first ←→ Long-form focused |
| Production | Simple/fast ←→ High production |
| Personality | Faceless/educational ←→ Personality-driven |
| Content Type | Evergreen/SEO ←→ Trending/timely |
| Monetization | AdSense ←→ Products/affiliate |

---

## Derivation Logic

| If Context Is... | Then Consider... |
|------------------|------------------|
| Tech tutorial + SEO goal | Long-form, searchable titles, chapters, evergreen content |
| Entertainment + personality | Trending topics, high energy, Shorts funnel to long-form |
| Low capacity + faceless | Screen recordings, voiceover, Shorts repurposed |
| Product sales goal | Educational long-form, affiliate mentions, pinned comments |
| New channel + discovery | Shorts heavy (3-7/week), long-form weekly for depth |

---

## TL;DR

| Component | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Thumbnail** | 50% of click decision |
| **Title** | 30% of click decision |
| **Hook** | First 30 sec = watch or bounce |
| **Retention** | Watch time = algorithm fuel |
| **SEO** | Discovery in search |

## Part 1: Thumbnails

### Thumbnail Formula

```markdown
## Elements That Work

1. **Face with emotion** — Surprised, excited, skeptical
2. **Contrast** — Bright colors, dark background
3. **Text** — 3-4 words MAX, large font
4. **Curiosity gap** — Show result, hide method
5. **Before/After** — Split design
6. **Numbers** — $100K, 10x, etc.
```

### Thumbnail Checklist

```markdown
## Before Publishing

- [ ] Visible at small size (mobile)
- [ ] Readable text
- [ ] Clear focal point
- [ ] Not cluttered
- [ ] Consistent with channel style
- [ ] Creates curiosity

## Technical
- 1280 x 720 (16:9)
- PNG or JPG
- Under 2MB
- Test at small size
```

|------|----------|

## Part 2: Titles

### Title Formulas

```markdown
## High-CTR Patterns

1. **Number + Result**
   "7 Habits That Made Me $100K"

2. **How To + Benefit**
   "How To Build a SaaS in 30 Days"

3. **This Changed Everything**
   "This Tool Changed How I Code"

4. **Mistake Format**
   "Stop Making This API Mistake"

5. **Controversy/Hot Take**
   "Why I Quit Using [Popular Thing]"

6. **Comparison**
   "Next.js vs Remix — Honest Review"

7. **Behind the Scenes**
   "How I Built [Thing] From Scratch"
```

### Title Optimization

```markdown
## Best Practices

- 50-60 characters max
- Front-load keywords
- Create curiosity gap
- Avoid clickbait (deliver on promise)
- A/B test with YouTube's feature
- Check thumbnail + title together
```

## Part 3: Hooks (First 30 Seconds)

### Hook Structure

```markdown
## The 30-Second Formula

0-3 sec: Pattern interrupt (visual or audio)
3-10 sec: Hook statement (why watch)
10-20 sec: Preview value (what they'll learn)
20-30 sec: Credibility + transition

## Example

"I made $50K from one YouTube video... [visual hook]
...and I'm going to show you exactly how, step by step.
By the end of this video, you'll have a complete system
for making money on YouTube without showing your face.
I've been doing this for 3 years, so let's dive in."
```

### Hook Mistakes

```markdown
## Don't Do This

❌ "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel..."
❌ Asking to like/subscribe first
❌ Channel updates/announcements
❌ Slow intro music
❌ Thanking them for clicking
❌ Explaining what you'll cover (too long)
```

## Part 4: YouTube Shorts

### Shorts Strategy

```markdown
## What Works

- 15-60 seconds optimal
- Hook in first second
- Vertical 9:16 format
- Text on screen
- Quick cuts, high energy
- Trending audio (if relevant)

## Content Ideas
- Clips from long videos
- Quick tips
- Behind the scenes
- Reactions
- POV content
- Tutorials (compressed)
```

### Shorts to Long-Form Funnel

```markdown
## Growth Strategy

1. Post Short with teaser content
2. End with: "Full tutorial on my channel"
3. Pin comment with video link
4. Profile links to main video

## Cross-Posting
- Same content can go to TikTok/Reels
- Adjust captions for platform
- Remove watermarks before cross-posting
```

## Part 5: SEO

### Keyword Research

```markdown
## Finding Keywords

1. **YouTube Search**
   - Type topic, note autocomplete
   - Check "related searches"

2. **Competitor Analysis**
   - What titles rank?
   - Check their tags (TubeBuddy/vidIQ)

3. **Tools**
   - TubeBuddy
   - vidIQ
   - Ahrefs (YouTube tab)
   - KeywordTool.io

## Long-Tail Strategy
"How to build a SaaS" → competitive
"How to build a SaaS with Next.js 2025" → less competitive
```

### On-Video SEO

```markdown
## Optimization Checklist

**Title**
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 5 words
- [ ] Under 60 characters

**Description**
- [ ] Keyword in first sentence
- [ ] 200+ words
- [ ] Timestamps/chapters
- [ ] Links and CTAs
- [ ] Related videos linked

**Tags**
- [ ] Primary keyword first
- [ ] Variations included
- [ ] 5-10 relevant tags

**Closed Captions**
- [ ] Upload accurate transcript
- [ ] YouTube auto-captions checked
```

## Part 6: Chapters and Timestamps

```markdown
## How to Add Chapters

In description, add:

0:00 - Introduction
1:23 - First Topic
3:45 - Second Topic
6:00 - Third Topic
8:30 - Conclusion

## Requirements
- First timestamp must be 0:00
- Minimum 3 chapters
- Each at least 10 seconds apart

## Benefits
- Better viewer experience
- Shows in search results
- Reduces bounce rate
- Google featured snippets
```

## Part 7: End Screens and Cards

### End Screen Strategy

```markdown
## Last 20 Seconds

Add:
1. Subscribe button
2. Best performing video
3. Newest video or playlist
4. Related content

## Placement
- Leave 20 sec of "outro" screen
- Don't cover important content
- Use consistent end screen template
```

### Cards Strategy

```markdown
## When to Use Cards

- Reference another video
- Link to playlist
- Promote channel
- Poll viewers

## Best Practices
- Don't overuse (1-2 per video)
- Place when relevant mentioned
- Avoid first 30 seconds
- Track click-through rate
```

## Part 8: Analytics

### Key Metrics

```markdown
## What to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| CTR (Click-Through Rate) | Thumbnail + title quality |
| AVD (Average View Duration) | Content quality |
| Watch Time | Algorithm ranking factor |
| Subscriber Growth | Channel health |
| Retention Graph | Where viewers drop |

## Benchmarks
- CTR: 5-10% good, 10%+ great
- AVD: 50%+ for short, 40%+ for long
- Retention: Check for drop-offs
```

### Retention Graph Analysis

```markdown
## Reading the Graph

**Sharp drop at 0:30**
→ Hook is weak, not delivering on promise

**Gradual decline**
→ Normal, content could be tighter

**Spike then drop**
→ Clickbait issue, not matching expectations

**Rise in middle**
→ People rewatching (good content)

**Drop at end**
→ Normal, add end screen here
```

## Part 9: Content Strategy

### Video Types

```markdown
## Content Mix

**Evergreen (60%)**
- Tutorials, how-tos
- Reviews, comparisons
- Educational deep-dives
- Searchable content

**Trending (20%)**
- News reactions
- New tool reviews
- Hot takes
- Timely content

**Personal (20%)**
- Behind the scenes
- Q&A
- Channel updates
- Community building
```

### Posting Schedule

```markdown
## Consistency

**Ideal Frequency**
- 1-2 long videos/week
- 3-7 Shorts/week

**Best Days**
Thursday, Friday, Saturday

**Best Times (EST)**
2 PM - 4 PM

## The 30-Video Rule
Commit to 30 videos before judging results.
Most channels take off between video 30-50.
```
